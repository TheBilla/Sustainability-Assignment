How to discuss this with your supplier
What this number actually means
Emissions intensity measures how many kilograms of greenhouse gas are produced per kilogram of beef harvested (kg CO₂-e/kg HSCW). A lower number means more efficient production. For example, this supplier's F27 figure means they produce that many kg of CO₂-equivalent for every kg of beef - improving this score means doing the same or more with less environmental impact. You don't need to explain the science; just say: "This is a measure of how efficiently your operation produces beef from an emissions perspective, and it's been improving industry-wide."
MLA verification - say this with confidence
This figure comes directly from the MLA Supplier Sustainability Program (SSP) and has been independently verified by Meat & Livestock Australia. If a supplier questions the data, you can state clearly: "This figure is sourced from your MLA SSP report - it's the same data MLA uses across the industry, not a Woolworths calculation."
Common supplier pushbacks - suggested responses
💬 "This data isn't accurate - our emissions are lower than this."
Suggested response: "I understand that concern. The figure comes directly from your MLA SSP submission, so it reflects the data your operation reported to MLA. If you believe there's an error, the best path is to work with MLA to update your SSP report - we'd be happy to accept a revised figure before the next cycle closes."
💬 "This isn't fair on smaller farms - we don't have the resources to compete."
Suggested response: "That's a fair point, and it's exactly why the EPS measures year-on-year improvement rather than an absolute benchmark. We're not asking you to match large operations - we're looking for progress relative to your own baseline. The MLA CN30 program also has funded advisory support specifically designed for smaller producers. We can connect you with that today."

Why enforcement credibility matters
A2 testing found that role-identity shift drives behaviour. If managers discover consequences are symbolic, the effect weakens quickly.
4 suppliers visibly lost premium shelf in F27, creating a credible signal across the full supplier community.
11 suppliers gained premium shelf, demonstrating the commercial reward is real and achievable.
The EPS measures each supplier against their own prior-year baseline, not an industry benchmark. Smaller producers are not structurally disadvantaged. Bottom-quartile suppliers receive MLA CN30 advisory support before any commercial consequence is applied.
